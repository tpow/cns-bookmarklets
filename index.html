What's this? Bookmarklets are “Smart Bookmarks”, tiny programs that expand the functionality of a website with the click of a bookmark. Bookmarklets work almost everywhere — PCs, Macs, tablets, even mobile phones — and with all major browsers including Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Internet Explorer, and Safari. The bookmarklets on this page are helpful when using the CampusNexus Student Web Client. Once you are signed in to the Web Client, click a bookmarklet just like you would a normal bookmark to trigger its action.

How do I save them? Before you can use a bookmarklet, you need to save it in your browser's bookmarks or favorites. Most browsers have a special toolbar for this that gives you convenient access to your bookmarks. Enable the bookmarks toolbar and then drag the bookmarklet button on to it. If you wish, you can then rename the bookmark.

Quick Links

Quickly switch to a module and tile for a student in the CampusNexus Student Web Client similiar to buttons on the Desktop Client's Custom Toolbar. To use this you must already be working with a student's record. This is just like expanding a module section and clicking on a tile (and sometimes on a tab after that) but can be faster and easier.

1. Choose what you want the bookmarklet to open. The bookmarklet button below updates as you change this form.

2. Drag quick link for to your bookmarks bar.

Tips:

  • Feel free to rename the bookmark.
  • To add a picture like the custom toolbar, many browsers let you use Emojis in the bookmark name.
  • You can use folders to organize your CampusNexus bookmarks.

Convenience

These bookmarklets are general-purpose. They can be handy when working with the Web Client.

Duplicate Tab

Duplicates the current page in a new tab. This makes it easy to copy a page so you can return to it later or to do comparisons.

Drag Duplicate Tab to your bookmarks bar.

Rename Tab

Lets you change the title of the web page window or tab. This might be useful if you're working with a lot of similarly named tabs.

Drag Rename Tab to your bookmarks bar.
